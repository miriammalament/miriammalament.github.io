About Me

I am an applied economist working with causal inference and large-scale data to answer real-world questions. I translate complex analysis into clear, decision-relevant insights for policy, organisations, and decision-makers.

My interests sit at the intersection of applied economics, public policy, and data-driven problem solving, with a focus on rigorous research design and transparent empirical methods.

Research Interests

Causal inference; program evaluation; policy analysis; panel and administrative data; machine learning for heterogeneity; reproducible research.
