About me

I use economic tools to make sense of how people and institutions respond to rules, incentives, and uncertainty — often in ways that standard assumptions miss.

My work focuses on applied questions at the intersection of policy and institutions, with an emphasis on participation decisions, regulatory thresholds, and equilibrium responses. I combine causal analysis with a strong focus on clarity and decision relevance.

I have experience across academic and applied research environments in Europe and the United States, including Yale University and now at the Ellison Institute of Technology, Oxford.

Featured - Master's thesis

Do Symbolic Penalties Work?

A five-cent fine that is almost never enforced still corresponds to a ~20% jump in voter turnout at age 18 in Argentina.

The project tests whether legal obligation can change behavior when deterrence is basically absent. Using the age-18 threshold, patterns are most consistent with the law acting as a social signal rather than a credible threat.

  • ~20% turnout jump
  • 15 national elections (2015–2023)
  • 5 cents fine (approx.)
