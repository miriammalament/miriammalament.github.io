Profile

I am an applied economist specialising in causal inference and large-scale data analysis, with experience translating complex empirical evidence into clear, decision-ready insights for senior stakeholders.

My work sits at the intersection of economics, data science, and public policy. I focus on answering high-stakes questions where evidence quality matters: what works, for whom, and at what cost.

I have worked with interdisciplinary teams across research, policy, and technology environments, contributing rigorous analysis to inform strategy, programme design, and evaluation.

Capabilities

Causal inference and programme evaluation · Experimental and quasi-experimental design · Panel and administrative data · Reproducible research pipelines · Applied machine learning for heterogeneity and targeting · Clear communication of technical results to non-technical audiences
