Featured project

Do symbolic penalties work?

A $0.05 fine that is almost never enforced still coincides with a ~20-point jump in turnout at age 18.

This project asks a simple question: can a legal obligation change behaviour even when enforcement is basically zero? Using Argentina’s compulsory voting threshold, I estimate a sharp jump in turnout at 18 and show patterns consistent with the law acting as a social signal rather than a deterrent.

What is “expressive compliance”?

Behaviour can change not because people fear punishment, but because the law signals a shared norm. In that view, even symbolic penalties can matter.

Macroeconomics · Expectations · 2023

Announced Stabilization Programs: Expectations and Macro Dynamics

When a stabilization plan is announced in advance, expectations move first. I study how that anticipation reshapes the transition path and the familiar boom–bust patterns.

Takeaway: timing and credibility can flip the textbook “surprise stabilization” dynamics.

Fiscal policy · Institutions · Feb 2023

Designing a Countercyclical Fiscal Fund

A practical blueprint for an anti-cyclical fiscal fund when revenues are volatile and politics makes smoothing hard.

The approach is deliberately implementable: it builds fund rules around real constraints (market access, credibility, political economy). (Co-authored with Mariano Fernández.)

Labour · Human capital · Argentina · 2023

Returns to Education and Human Capital Investment: Evidence from Argentina

How large is the payoff to schooling in Argentina, and has it changed? I estimate returns to education using household survey microdata and track patterns over time.

Emphasis: consistent estimates across waves and clear results by education level.
