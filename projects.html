Featured

Do Symbolic Penalties Work? Evidence from Argentina’s Compulsory Voting Law

A $0.05 fine that is almost never enforced still lines up with a ~20pp jump in turnout at age 18.

The project asks whether legal obligation can change behaviour when deterrence is basically absent. Using the age-18 threshold, the patterns are most consistent with the law acting as a social signal rather than a credible threat.

  • ~20pp turnout jump
  • 15 national elections (2015–2023)
  • $0.05 real fine (approx.)
