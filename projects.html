Projects

I build empirical evidence for practical policy and strategy questions: when rules change, who responds, by how much, and why. My work uses administrative and survey microdata, clean identification, and clear takeaways.

Featured — Master’s thesis

Do Symbolic Penalties Work?

A 5-cent fine that is almost never enforced still lines up with a ~20% jump in voter turnout at age 18 in Argentina.

The project tests whether legal obligation can change behaviour when deterrence is basically absent. Using the age-18 threshold, patterns are most consistent with the law acting as a social signal rather than a credible threat.

  • ~20% turnout jump
  • 15 national elections (2015–2023)
  • 5¢ real fine (approx.)

Announced Stabilization Programs: Expectations and Macro Dynamics

Problem: Stabilisation plans are often evaluated as “surprises,” but many are announced early.

Approach: We study how anticipation and credibility reshape transition dynamics in money-based and exchange-rate-based stabilisations, focusing on expectations.

Takeaway: Timing changes the mechanism. When agents move first, the predicted boom–bust pattern can reverse.

Designing a Countercyclical Fiscal Fund

Problem: Volatile revenues and political constraints make smoothing difficult in practice.

Approach: We develop a fund design that makes constraints explicit—market access, credibility, and political economy—so the rule can be implemented rather than idealised.

Takeaway: The framework translates “save in booms, spend in busts” into rules that remain credible under real-world incentives and shocks.

Returns to Education and Human Capital Investment: Evidence from Argentina

Problem: How large is the payoff to schooling in Argentina, and how stable is it over time?

Approach: I estimate returns to education using household survey microdata, harmonising definitions across waves to produce clean, comparable results.

Takeaway: Consistent estimates help interpret wage inequality and clarify the incentives households face when investing in education.
