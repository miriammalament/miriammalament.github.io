The power of symbolic law

A $0.05 fine that is almost never enforced still coincides with a ~20-point jump in turnout at age 18.

My master’s thesis studies Argentina’s compulsory voting threshold as a clean test of whether legal obligation can change behaviour even when deterrence is basically absent. The evidence is most consistent with expressive compliance — the idea that the law works as a normative signal.

What is “expressive compliance”?

The law can change behaviour not because people fear punishment, but because it signals a shared norm (“this is what citizens do”). In that view, even symbolic penalties can matter.
